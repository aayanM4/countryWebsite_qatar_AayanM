Dishes from Qatar

Harees

a hearty, ancient Middle Eastern porridge made from slow-cooked wheat and meat (like lamb or chicken) until it forms a smooth, creamy consistency, often flavored with ghee and spices like cinnamon

Lokma

deep-fried dough balls that are golden and crispy on the outside while remaining light and airy on the inside.

Kabsa

a traditional, fragrant mixed rice dish

Balaleet

a popular sweet and savory breakfast

Madrouba

a traditional, comforting Middle Eastern savory porridge popular in Qatar

Saloona

a classic Arabian stew popular in Qatar,

Shawarma

a popular Middle Eastern street food featuring thinly sliced, spiced meat (chicken, lamb, beef) slow-roasted on a vertical spit, known for its aromatic, savory flavor from spices like cumin, cinnamon, and paprika, served hot in a wrap or on a plate with toppings like pickles, onions, hummus, and tahini sauce

Tharid

A dish of rich meat and vegetable stew served over broken flatbread
